Allegory in the Book of Mormon

Definition

An allegory is an extended metaphor where characters, events, and settings symbolically represent deeper truths or concepts.

Historical Context

Allegories appear in biblical texts (e.g., the Vineyard in Isaiah 5) and were used to convey complex spiritual ideas in accessible narratives.
